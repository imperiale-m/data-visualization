Bar chart

Bar charts are a good way to compare values quickly and accurately. In this case I used a horizontal bar chart because this way it's easier to read the species. Celtis Australis is highlighted because I used it in the title and the bars are sorted for easier comparing.

Stacked bar chart

Stacked bar charts are a variation of bar charts that subdivides the data within each category. In this case we are looking at the top 5 species (+ others) for each district. In this case the categories sum to the total, so it's easier to compare them in absolute terms. It may be more difficult to compare the segments within though.

Small multiples

Small multiples are great if we want to compare values across the districts in a certain category. The side effect is that is usually harder (or impossible) to see the total. In this case I had to remove both the total and the other category to make it more readable. We can also sort a category to highlight it, but I didn't.

Waffle chart

Waffle charts are a way to display part of a whole. Each square represents more or less 1% of the total, because there will be some errors due to quantization.
